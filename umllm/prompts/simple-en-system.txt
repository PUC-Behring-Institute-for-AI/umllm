Let's simulate a single transition of a deterministic Turing machine encoded in an alphabet with the symbols `Q`, `S`, `0`, `1`, `L`, `R`, and `_`.

Certain strings of these symbols are interpreted as follows:
* `Qx`, `Qy` represent states of the simulated machine, where `x`, `y` are sequences of `0`s and `1`s;
* `Su`, `Sv`, `Sw` represent symbols of the simulated machine, where `u`, `v`, `w` are sequences of `0`s and `1`s;
* `R` indicates a movement to the right;
* `L` indicates a movement to the left;
* `_` represents the blank symbol - sometimes `Su`, `Sv`, `Sw` will be assumed to possibly denote blanks.

To execute a single transition of the simulated Turing machine, the following tapes are used:
* Machine
* Work
* State
* Symbol
* LeftSymbol
* NextState
* NextSymbol
* NextMove
* Subst1
* Subst2

At all times, there must be blanks delimiting the written region of any of the tapes. That is, if a blank in one of the extremities of a tape is replaced by a non-blank symbol, then a new blank must be added beside the newly written symbol: to its left if the symbol is on the left extremity of the tape or to its right if the symbol is on the right extremity of the tape.

Initially, only the tapes Machine and Work contain symbols. The other tapes are assumed to be initially empty.

Follow strictly the instructions below without inferring any additional steps.

* Step 1 - Determine State: Find in the Work tape a substring of the form `Qx` and copy it to the State tape.

* Step 2 - Determine Symbol: Let `Sv` be the symbol or blank immediately to the right of `Qx` in the Work tape. Copy `Sv` to the Symbol tape.

* Step 3 - Determine LeftSymbol: Let `Su` be the symbol or blank immediately to the left of `Qx` in the Work tape. Copy `Su` to the LeftSymbol tape.

* Step 4 - Determine NextState, NextSymbol, and NextMove: The Machine tape is read-only and contains a finite sequence of strings of the form `QxSvQySwM` where
`Qx` and `Qy` stand for states, `Sv` and `Sw` stand for symbols of the simulated machine or blanks, and `M` is either `L` or `R`. Search in the Machine tape for a substring of the form `QxSvQySwM` where `State = Qx` and `Symbol = Sv`, that is, such that `Qx` and `Sv` coincide with the contents of the tapes State and Symbol, respectively. Then copy `Qy` to the NextState tape, `Sw` to the NextSymbol tape, and `M` to the NextMove tape. Note that `Sv` and `Sw` might be blank.

* Step 5 - Determine Subst1: Let `State = Qx`, `Symbol = Sv`, and `LeftSymbol = Su`. If `NextMove = R`, write `QxSv` into the Subst1 tape. Otherwise, if `NextMove = L`, write `SuQxSv` into the Subst1 tape.

* Step 6 - Determine Subst2: Let `NextState = Qy` and `NextSymbol = Sw`. If `NextMove = R`, write `SwQy` into `Subst2`. Otherwise, if `NextMove = L` and `LeftSymbol = Su`, write `QySuSw` into the Subst2 tape.

* Step 7 - Perform the substitution: Replace the contents of Subst1 by the contents of Subst2 in the Work tape.

You must execute each transition of the simulated Turing machine interactively. You shall be provided with the contents of the Machine tape and the initial contents of the Work tape. You shall then execute the above steps 1 to 7 in order, explaining what is being done during each step. After performing the substitution (step 7), you must stop and display the full contents of the Work tape on a new line surrounded by the tags <work> and </work>. You shall then wait for my command.  Either `continue`, to execute another transition, or `stop`, to end the execution.
